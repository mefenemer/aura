Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 27, 2026

1. Our Commitment to Data Privacy

At Aura-Assist, we understand that you are trusting us with your business operations and data. We operate on a principle of strict data isolation: your business data belongs to you, and we do not use your information to train public AI models.

2. Data We Collect

We collect information necessary to build and manage your digital assistants, including:

  • Contact details provided during onboarding.
  • Workflow instructions and business process logic.
  • Data processed by your assistants (e.g., email content, lead form data) solely for the purpose of executing the tasks you have requested.

3. Data Isolation and Security

Your digital assistant's "memory" is stored in a secure, isolated vault specific to your account. We employ enterprise-grade encryption for all data at rest and in transit. We ensure that your proprietary information—such as your customer lists, pricing, or internal communications—remains confidential and is not shared with third-party model providers for training purposes.

4. How We Use Your Data

We use your data exclusively to:

  • Build, test, and deploy your custom digital assistants.
  • Monitor assistant performance to ensure reliability.
  • Communicate with you regarding updates to your workflows or service notifications.

5. Your Control

You maintain full ownership of your workflows and your proprietary business data. You may request the deletion of your data or the cancellation of your assistant at any time.

Migration Policy:

Upon request, we will provide you with a plain-text export of your assistant's job instructions and workflow logic. Please note that while you own the logic, the specific technical architecture and integrations we have built are proprietary to Aura-Assist. We do not provide direct migration of technical infrastructure or "plug-and-play" files to third-party agencies. If you choose to switch providers, we will facilitate the handover by providing your documented instructions, and upon final confirmation, we will securely purge your account-specific assistant memory and associated data.

6. Google API Services — Limited Use Disclosure

Aura-Assist's use of information received from Google APIs adheres to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements.

Specifically, data obtained through Google API connections (including Gmail, Google Calendar, and Google Drive) will only be used to provide or improve user-facing features that are prominent in Aura-Assist's user interface. This data will not be used for advertising, will not be transferred to third parties except as necessary to provide the Services or as required by law, and will not be used to train AI foundation models. Human access to this data is restricted to cases where it is necessary to provide the Services, to comply with applicable law, or where you have given express consent.

7. International Data Transfers

Aura-Assist is a UK-based data controller. When you use the Services, your data may be transferred to and processed in the United States by the following sub-processors:

  • Anthropic / OpenAI — foundation AI model inference. Transfer basis: Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) under UK IDTA and EU Commission Decision 2021/914, supplemented by a Transfer Impact Assessment (TIA). Contractual no-training-on-API-data clause confirmed in executed DPA.
  • Stripe, Inc. — payment processing and subscription management. Transfer basis: UK–US Data Privacy Framework adequacy (confirmed current). Stripe DPA executed.
  • Resend — transactional email delivery. Transfer basis: Standard Contractual Clauses. Resend DPA / SCCs executed.
  • Netlify, Inc. — serverless hosting and edge functions. Transfer basis: Netlify DPA (available at netlify.com/dpa) covering UK/EU GDPR. Executed DPA in place.
  • Neon / PostgreSQL host — managed PostgreSQL database. Database is hosted in an EU region (eu-west). No US transfer for stored personal data.

For transfers to the US under SCCs, we have conducted a Transfer Impact Assessment (TIA) evaluating: (1) the US legal framework (FISA 702 / EO 14086 safeguards), (2) each provider's DPA commitments, and (3) technical supplementary measures including prompt PII minimisation, contractual no-training clauses, and data minimisation on context sent to LLM APIs. The TIA is retained as part of our Article 30 Records of Processing Activities and is available to users on request.

A full sub-processor register listing each processor's name, data transferred, legal transfer basis, region, and last-reviewed date is available on request by emailing privacy@aura-assist.com. We will notify you 14 days before adding any new sub-processor that handles your personal data.

8. Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy periodically to reflect changes in our service or legal requirements. We will notify all active subscribers of significant updates.
